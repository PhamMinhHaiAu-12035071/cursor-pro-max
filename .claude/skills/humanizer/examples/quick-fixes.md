# Quick Fix Examples by Pattern

One-line before/after for each of the 24 AI writing patterns. Use as a fast-scan reference.

---

## Content Patterns

**1. Significance inflation**
- Before: "marking a pivotal moment in the evolution of regional statistics"
- After: "established in 1989 to collect and publish regional statistics"

**2. Notability claims**
- Before: "cited in The New York Times, BBC, Financial Times, and The Hindu"
- After: "In a 2024 New York Times interview, she argued that..."

**3. Superficial -ing analyses**
- Before: "symbolizing Texas bluebonnets, reflecting the community's deep connection"
- After: "The architect said these were chosen to reference local bluebonnets"

**4. Promotional language**
- Before: "Nestled within the breathtaking region... vibrant town with rich cultural heritage"
- After: "a town in the Gonder region, known for its weekly market"

**5. Vague attributions**
- Before: "Experts believe it plays a crucial role in the regional ecosystem"
- After: "supports several endemic fish species, according to a 2019 survey by..."

**6. Formulaic challenges**
- Before: "Despite these challenges... continues to thrive as an integral part"
- After: "Traffic congestion increased after 2015 when three new IT parks opened"

---

## Language and Grammar Patterns

**7. AI vocabulary**
- Before: "Additionally... enduring testament... culinary landscape, showcasing"
- After: "also includes camel meat... Pasta dishes remain common"

**8. Copula avoidance**
- Before: "Gallery 825 serves as... features four spaces and boasts over"
- After: "Gallery 825 is... The gallery has four rooms totaling"

**9. Negative parallelisms**
- Before: "It's not just about the beat; it's part of the aggression"
- After: "The heavy beat adds to the aggressive tone"

**10. Rule of three**
- Before: "keynote sessions, panel discussions, and networking opportunities"
- After: "talks and panels. There's also time for informal networking"

**11. Synonym cycling**
- Before: "The protagonist... The main character... The central figure... The hero"
- After: "The protagonist faces many challenges but eventually triumphs"

**12. False ranges**
- Before: "from the singularity of the Big Bang to the grand cosmic web"
- After: "covers the Big Bang, star formation, and current theories"

---

## Style Patterns

**13. Em dash overuse**
- Before: "by Dutch institutions---not by the people---yet this continues---even in"
- After: "by Dutch institutions, not by the people, yet this continues in"

**14. Boldface overuse**
- Before: "**OKRs**, **KPIs**, and **Business Model Canvas (BMC)**"
- After: "OKRs, KPIs, and visual strategy tools like the Business Model Canvas"

**15. Inline-header lists**
- Before: "- **User Experience:** improved... - **Performance:** enhanced..."
- After: "improves the interface, speeds up load times, and adds encryption"

**16. Title case headings**
- Before: "## Strategic Negotiations And Global Partnerships"
- After: "## Strategic negotiations and global partnerships"

**17. Emojis**
- Before: ":rocket: **Launch Phase:** :bulb: **Key Insight:** :white_check_mark: **Next Steps:**"
- After: "The product launches in Q3. User research showed a preference for simplicity."

**18. Curly quotes**
- Before: He said \u201cthe project is on track\u201d
- After: He said "the project is on track"

---

## Communication Patterns

**19. Chatbot artifacts**
- Before: "Here is an overview. I hope this helps! Let me know if..."
- After: "The French Revolution began in 1789 when financial crisis and..."

**20. Knowledge-cutoff disclaimers**
- Before: "While specific details are not extensively documented in readily available sources"
- After: "The company was founded in 1994, according to its registration documents"

**21. Sycophantic tone**
- Before: "Great question! You're absolutely right that this is complex"
- After: "The economic factors you mentioned are relevant here"

---

## Filler and Hedging

**22. Filler phrases**
- Before: "In order to achieve this goal" / "Due to the fact that"
- After: "To achieve this" / "Because"

**23. Excessive hedging**
- Before: "could potentially possibly be argued that it might have some effect"
- After: "The policy may affect outcomes"

**24. Generic conclusions**
- Before: "The future looks bright. Exciting times lie ahead"
- After: "The company plans to open two more locations next year"
